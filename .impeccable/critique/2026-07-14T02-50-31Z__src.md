Scope: every public surface and all non-blog-post copy. Blog article bodies are excluded. Existing logo-derived colors are fixed.

## Anti-pattern verdict

### LLM assessment

The site does not look carelessly generated, but it does look assembled from a disciplined landing-page kit. The repeated `PageHero → RainbowDivider → stacked cards → generic tail CTA` grammar, tiny uppercase kicker, huge condensed uppercase heading, centered lead and orange pill CTA makes adoption, donation, foster, FAQ, contact, collaborators and 404 feel like content substitutions inside one template. It is coherent but insufficiently art-directed. The organization has a distinctive subject, local imagery and an unusually humane operating model; the interface does not exploit them enough.

### Deterministic scan

The detector returned 20 findings: 2 warnings and 18 advisories. Counts: 8 undocumented colors, 8 off-ramp font sizes, 2 off-scale radii, 1 undocumented font and 1 apparent broken image. The apparent broken image at `src/components/SharedGalleryLightbox.astro:29` is an intentional JS-populated shell, not evidence of a shipped broken photo. Some black-alpha literals and fluid radii are defensible, but the scan still reveals real token/documentation drift. The most actionable items are repeated error-red literals, eight ad-hoc type sizes and gallery controls below 44px on the “Por qué galgos” route.

No visual overlay was injected. Browser evidence came from fresh headless Playwright rendering and screenshots.

## Overall impression

Brigada already sounds more humane and practical than most rescue organizations. The site explains difficult choices without guilt and contains real proof: named dogs, local photos, structured profiles, operating details and success cases. Its biggest opportunity is to stop presenting the organization as a set of well-written sections and start presenting each galgo as the protagonist of a visible route from rescue to home.

The redesign should not add decoration. It should remove roughly one third of the repeated wrappers, card shells, kickers, dividers and duplicate CTAs, then invest that space in named photography, contextual proof, dramatic pacing and clearer decisions.

## What is working

- The core voice is warm, direct and non-coercive. “No necesitas tener todo resuelto…” lowers the barrier to contact without weaponizing suffering.
- Dog profiles carry actual decision information—photos, age, weight, current need, temperament, contact and application—not sentimental filler.
- Accessibility foundations are intentional: shared components, semantic headings, live regions, focus states, meaningful alt text and large primary controls.
- “Brigada cubre / Tú aportas” is the best conversion copy on the site because it resolves a concrete foster objection immediately.
- The site has enough real material to become visually distinctive without stock art or invented claims.

## Priority issues

### [P1] Adoptable dogs are absent from the homepage narrative

The homepage hero leads with organizational operations, then shows success stories, mission, statistics, breed education, rescue process, three help cards and donation. Visitors never meet the dogs who need families now. Completed work is celebrated before current need is shown.

**Why it matters:** adoption is the primary conversion, but it is not the visual protagonist. Emotion has no named object and motivated visitors must navigate away before meeting a dog.

**Fix:** make one current dog the hero, follow immediately with a live “Hoy esperan familia” roster, and move success stories after process/proof. Give the featured dog one primary action—“Conoce a [nombre]”—with “Ver todos los galgos” as a quiet secondary route.

**Suggested command:** `/impeccable shape homepage`

### [P1] One visual template is doing the work of many journeys

Adoption, foster, donation, “Por qué galgos,” contact, FAQ, collaborators, blog listing and 404 share essentially the same opening and section grammar. Most secondary heroes are text-only despite a rich asset library.

**Why it matters:** consistency has crossed into sameness. Visitors cannot feel the difference between meeting a dog, deciding to foster, trusting a donation route and reading a reference page. The brand becomes interchangeable with a polished NGO template.

**Fix:** art-direct each route according to its job. Adoption becomes a living roster; foster becomes a domestic week-in-the-life; donation becomes a current case plus transparent money path; “Por qué galgos” becomes a photographic field essay; contact becomes an intent router; 404 becomes a silhouette-led brand moment.

**Suggested command:** `/impeccable shape full site`

### [P1] Homepage hierarchy and pacing are overloaded

Three hero actions compete at once, followed by nine large blocks with similar weight. Mission, statistics and the rescue stepper repeat the organization story in three formats; HelpCards repeats the three-way action choice from the hero.

**Why it matters:** visitors must understand the mission, dogs, breed, rescue mechanics, trust, adoption, fostering and donations simultaneously. Mobile home length measured roughly 9,047px.

**Fix:** establish one dominant action per fold and one narrative arc: dog → current need → fit → how Brigada helps → proof → alternate ways to act. Collapse mission/statistics/process into fewer, evidence-led modules.

**Suggested command:** `/impeccable distill homepage`

### [P1] Adoption cards ask for too many decisions too early

Cards can expose “Postular,” “Más información” and “Ver ficha” alongside chips and descriptive content. The hero also asks visitors to postulate before they see a dog.

**Why it matters:** repeated competing actions create a long tab order, encourage low-context applications and make the profile seem optional when it should be the conversion bridge.

**Fix:** make photo/name lead to the profile and keep one card CTA: “Conoce a [nombre].” Reserve “Iniciar postulación por [nombre]” and “Preguntar por [nombre] en WhatsApp” for the profile. Carry the dog’s name and image into the external form handoff.

**Suggested command:** `/impeccable clarify adoption journey`

### [P1] Donation trust is asserted more than demonstrated

The page uses bank data, eSponsor, suggested amounts, impact cards, statistics and four large “En qué no gastamos” cards. Claims such as “El aporte va completo a los galgos,” “No recibimos financiamiento estatal,” “100+ rescates” and “Los números que nos avalan” lack a visible period, source or reporting artifact. The transfer route is marked recommended while recurring support is also promoted.

**Why it matters:** defensive claims introduce suspicion and absolute language creates reputational risk. Visitors need proof beside the payment choice, not further down the page.

**Fix:** lead with one real current case; show a dated need or funding goal, recent spending example, legal identity/RUT, update date, receipt/confirmation path and a compact transparency disclosure. Rename choices plainly: “Transferencia” and “Aporte mensual con eSponsor.” State fees/cancellation where applicable. Treat amounts as dated estimates.

**Suggested command:** `/impeccable shape donation page`

### [P1] The brand proposition is descriptive rather than ownable

“Rescatamos, rehabilitamos y reubicamos galgos en Chile” is accurate SEO copy but could belong to any specialist rescue. The differentiator—foster-based operation, case-by-case matching and accompaniment from first alert through post-adoption—is buried.

**Why it matters:** the visitor remembers the category, not Brigada’s specific promise.

**Fix:** lead visibly with accompaniment and safe matching: “Del aviso a una familia segura, seguimos presentes en cada etapa.” Keep functional rescue language in supporting and metadata copy.

**Suggested command:** `/impeccable clarify brand proposition`

### [P1] There is no first-class trust/about destination

Legal identity, volunteer team, founding year, no-shelter operating model and nationwide claim are scattered. “Colaboradores” has top-level prominence while “Nuestro trabajo” or “Quiénes somos” does not exist.

**Why it matters:** adopters and donors need to know who makes decisions, where the organization operates and how accountability works.

**Fix:** add “Nuestro trabajo” covering team, legal status, operating model, geography, interregional logistics, governance, dated milestones, financial transparency and partner roles. Move collaborators into this proof system.

**Suggested command:** `/impeccable craft trust page`

### [P1] Foster commitment is internally inconsistent

One surface says availability can be “desde un día,” while FAQ says “desde unas semanas hasta unos pocos meses.” A one-night emergency placement is not the same service as fostering.

**Why it matters:** this can produce unsuitable leads and weaken trust at a high-stakes handoff.

**Fix:** define the actual minimum; distinguish emergency/overnight help if it exists; explain transport, supplies, veterinary appointments, handoff and early-exit protocol. Merge the page’s two process sequences.

**Suggested command:** `/impeccable clarify hogar temporal`

### [P2] Mobile journeys are too long and lose action context

Measured mobile heights included donation 8,853px, why-galgos 8,147px, adoption 7,439px, FAQ 7,415px and foster 6,049px. High-intent pages require long backtracking to act.

**Fix:** add a compact sticky action bar only on profiles, foster and donation; use progressive disclosure for reference content; surface contextual FAQ excerpts at the point of doubt.

**Suggested command:** `/impeccable adapt conversion pages`

### [P2] Reassurance is repeated until it loses specificity

Variants of “No necesitas tener todo resuelto” and “seguimos acompañándote” appear across most routes. Defensive phrases introduce “juzgarte,” “burocrático” and “barreras” before visitors raise those concerns.

**Fix:** let each page resolve one anxiety. Adoption: compatibility without pressure. Foster: cost and logistics. Donation: traceability. Contact: response time. Replace “no para juzgarte” with “Estas preguntas nos ayudan a encontrar una convivencia segura.”

**Suggested command:** `/impeccable clarify site copy`

### [P2] Consent UI dominates the first mobile viewport

The cookie banner obscures the lower hero and CTA area and becomes the strongest persistent element until resolved.

**Fix:** preserve equal comprehension and accessible rejection while reducing footprint. Use explicit “Aceptar analítica” / “Rechazar analítica,” tighter copy and a quieter, non-modal mobile treatment.

**Suggested command:** `/impeccable distill cookie banner`

### [P2] Some controls miss the site’s own touch-target standard

“Por qué galgos” gallery dots measured 18×18 and previous/next controls 34×34, below the 44px target. No page-level horizontal overflow was found across 22 route/viewport combinations.

**Fix:** preserve the visual dot size but expand the clickable box to at least 44×44; align all shared gallery controls.

**Suggested command:** `/impeccable audit galleries`

## Persona red flags

### Daniela — first-time adopter in a Santiago apartment

She meets three competing homepage actions before any current dog, then must navigate to adoption, parse multiple actions per card and leave the site for a generic form. Location, compatibility, time alone, cat fit and ongoing costs are not consistently surfaced. She may submit prematurely or abandon because she cannot compare fit confidently.

### Camila — willing but anxious foster volunteer

She is reassured by “Brigada cubre / Tú aportas” but sees contradictory duration claims and two process explanations. She still does not know transport responsibility, appointment logistics or what happens if the placement must end. The page asks for a form before fully bounding the commitment.

### Rodrigo — skeptical recurring donor

He sees a vague “Voy a apoyar un galgo” CTA, a recommended transfer card, a separately branded monthly option and absolute claims without dates or a spending artifact. Four negative-spend cards increase suspicion. He wants a recent case, legal identity, platform-fee explanation and an update date beside the decision.

### Valentina — mobile visitor arriving from Instagram

The consent banner occupies much of her first viewport. She then scrolls thousands of pixels through repeated section grammar and loses access to context-specific actions. Small gallery controls and long backtracking make one-handed use unnecessarily costly.

## Page-by-page review

### Navigation and footer

Reframe around intent: `Galgos · Adoptar · Acoger · Donar · Conocer la Brigada`, with secondary routes in “Más” or the footer. Shorten the persistent CTA to “Donar.” Keep the footer legal identity and add the updated brand-color galgo silhouette as one confident signature, not a repeated motif.

### Homepage

The strongest visual composition is the existing desktop portrait plus direct headline, but the subject should be a named current dog. Remove the three-way hero CTA; show current dogs before success stories. Replace generic values (“Compasión, Responsabilidad, Transparencia, Comunidad”) with operational proof. Replace “Los números que nos avalan” with a dated, humble proof statement and distinguish rescues from adoptions.

### Adoption listing

Make “Los galgos que hoy esperan familia” the true visual opening and remove the pre-roster application CTA. Stop random ordering; use editorial urgency or fit criteria transparently. Show match count/reset. Use parallel filters (“Todos, Machos, Hembras, Adultos, Cachorros”) and prioritize fit chips over low-value facts.

### Dog profiles

Treat `currentNeed` as a prominent state, not another chip. Rename sections “Cómo es [nombre],” “Qué hogar le ayudaría” and “Lo que necesita ahora.” Replace the repeated generic full process with concise reassurance and related dogs. Keep a mobile action bar and dog-specific form context.

### Hogar temporal

Open with a real dog resting in an ordinary home. Move “Brigada cubre / Tú aportas” directly below the hero. Turn requirements into a quick eligibility check. Merge duplicated process sequences and explain the form time, required information and response sequence.

### Donation

Use a current dog and need above the fold. Clarify whether one-time transfer or monthly support is strategically primary. Move proof beside the methods. Replace the large negative-spend grid with one disclosure and a real spending breakdown. Date suggested amounts and clarify pooled versus case-specific funds. Correct “a traves” to “a través.”

### Por qué galgos

Turn the page into a photographic field essay: brief harm context, recovery, then ordinary family life. Replace negative framing (“No son perros difíciles”) and indebted anthropomorphism (“profundamente agradecidos”) with observable behavior and case-by-case nuance. Add credible Chile-specific context.

### Contact

Ask intent first, then recommend WhatsApp, form or email. Do not present three interchangeable channel cards plus a form. Publish honest response times and an emergency disclaimer if applicable. Give field-specific validation and recovery. Success should offer a relevant next step, not only home.

### FAQ

Add category jump routes and embed key answers on conversion pages. Add geography, duration, health status, ongoing cost, alone-time, leash safety, trial period, reporting danger and response-time questions. Rename “GARANTÍAS” to “ACOMPAÑAMIENTO DESPUÉS DE ADOPTAR.” Avoid guaranteeing “el galgo en tu sillón.”

### Collaborators

Stop randomizing. Group by contribution and explain what each relationship enables. Feature one concrete joint outcome. Replace corporate partnership phrasing with “Cuéntanos qué puedes aportar y coordinamos una ayuda concreta.”

### Blog listing/chrome

Keep article bodies untouched. Reframe the listing around utility—adoption, convivencia, rescate, salud—and demote count/RSS/update metadata. Add relevant, lightweight routes back to adoption/foster content.

### 404

Do not reuse the generic PageHero. Use the colored silhouette, one line of personality and three exits: current galgos, home, contact. Test a genuinely nonexistent production-like URL; slashless `/404` showed Astro’s dev diagnostic due trailing-slash behavior.

### Cookie/legal

Keep the legal experience quiet and explicit. Replace “LEGAL” with “TU PRIVACIDAD” or no kicker. Consent choices should name analytics directly.

## Redesign direction — “Del rescate a casa”

Use the physical metaphor of a rescue route ending in an ordinary Chilean home—not a shelter campaign or editorial magazine.

- Fixed brand colors become directional roles: cyan guides; magenta marks urgent/story moments; orange is reserved for the primary action; green/purple mark real process states. Remove the rainbow divider as default punctuation.
- Let decisive, named dog photography carry the interface. Use one dominant image per fold: portrait, leash walk, rest at home, family context. Avoid ornamental stock imagery.
- Keep the condensed display voice for dog names and major promises, but stop making every heading a poster. Use sentence case and calmer body hierarchy for practical content.
- Build asymmetric, image-led sections and vary pacing by route. Fewer cards; more editorial pairings, full-bleed images, calm narrow reading columns and proof embedded next to decisions.
- Add one page-load/photo choreography or route-line progression where it serves the story. Never fade every section identically; provide reduced-motion alternatives.
- Recolor the low-poly galgo silhouette to the exact logo palette if used colored, and reserve it for footer, 404 and at most one editorial transition.

### Recommended homepage sequence

1. Named current galgo: “[Nombre] busca una casa donde bajar la guardia.”
2. “Conoce a [nombre]” plus quiet “Ver todos los galgos.”
3. Live adoptable roster.
4. Three compact routes—adoptar, acoger, donar—with adoption visibly primary.
5. Behavior-led “¿Cómo sería vivir con un galgo?” fit module.
6. Team, recovery and post-adoption proof embedded in photography.
7. Foster story plus “Brigada cubre / Tú aportas.”
8. Current donation case with a clear, dated money path.
9. One success transition showing life after adoption.
10. Human closing invitation and direct WhatsApp.

## Minor observations

- Detector token drift: error colors, type sizes, radii and fallback font should be documented or tokenized.
- Inline/footer link boxes under 44px are not automatically failures when spacing exceptions apply; audit them rather than mass-enlarging text.
- Lazy images and the empty lightbox shell produced false-positive image metrics; no failed image requests were observed.
- Dev transfers of roughly 2.1–2.4MB are not a production performance verdict. Run production Lighthouse after redesign.
- Reproduce the transient black mobile contact navbar before filing it as a defect.

## Questions to settle before implementation

1. Is adoption unequivocally the homepage’s primary conversion, with fostering second and donation third, or should current capacity change that order dynamically?
2. Which donation route should be strategically recommended: one-time transfer or recurring eSponsor support?
3. What foster durations and emergency arrangements are actually supported today?
4. What dated financial/operational proof can Brigada publish without creating an unsustainable reporting burden?
5. Which current dog has the strongest, most representative photo set for the new homepage hero?
