## Anti-Patterns Verdict

The real dog photography, Chilean voice, low-poly mark, and condensed typography make the site recognisable rather than generic AI output. The remaining tell is repetition: oversized all-caps display type, magenta rules, black offsets, and saturated bands recur often enough to make distinct decision moments feel like one campaign template.

The deterministic scan is clean: 0 findings. This resolves earlier documentation/token drift and the intentional dynamic gallery-image false positive. No visual overlay was available; responsive Playwright captures were used as browser evidence.

## Overall Impression

The site is emotionally credible and technically disciplined. Its best opportunity is not more visual energy; it is making high-stakes choices feel calmer, clearer, and more personally matched.

## What's Working

- Real dog photography makes the cause immediate and avoids generic NGO presentation.
- CTA color roles are generally legible: orange for a decision, cyan for a secondary path, magenta for editorial emphasis.
- Dog cards make photo, name, need, facts, and next action quick to scan.

## Priority Issues

### P1 — Adoption filtering does not foreground current need

The PRD calls for adoption/foster/current-need filters, yet the initial experience prioritises age. A visitor who can only foster cannot make the most important matching choice first. Restore current-need filters as the primary decision and keep age secondary. Suggested command: `/impeccable clarify`.

### P1 — Mobile consent competes with the dog-card decision

The consent layer is compliant but occupies the bottom action zone on 390px screens. Reduce its mobile footprint while retaining equally clear accept and reject choices. Suggested command: `/impeccable polish`.

### P1 — The visual register is too consistently forceful

Poster-scale uppercase headings, thick underlines, black offsets, and saturated bands occur across adoption and donation. Reserve the maximal treatment for true hero moments; browsing, reassurance, and proof should be quieter. Suggested command: `/impeccable quieter`.

### P2 — Donation leads with payment plumbing

Transfer details and legal proof establish trust but arrive before tangible impact. Lead with concrete outcomes and the volunteer/no-state-funding rationale, then use bank data as evidence. Suggested command: `/impeccable layout`.

### P2 — Desktop navigation hides reassurance routes

FAQ and contact underpin the project promise that people can ask before deciding, but they are absent from the visible desktop navigation. Surface a compact reassurance path or make it explicit within La Brigada. Suggested command: `/impeccable clarify`.

## Persona Red Flags

- **Jordan, first-time adopter:** cannot readily distinguish a foster need from adoption suitability before engaging more deeply.
- **Camila, mobile visitor:** meets consent friction at the moment a dog-card CTA should feel easy to act on.
- **Diego, cautious donor:** sees payment mechanics before the concrete outcomes that justify the gift.

## Minor Observations

- Photography can carry more quiet space; not every section needs the same headline treatment.
- The magenta underline is strong as a signature, weak when repeated as default hierarchy.

## Questions to Consider

- Is age intentionally replacing the PRD-required current-need filtering? If so, update the product contract; if not, treat it as a product mismatch.
- Which decision should feel most emotionally charged: meeting a dog, asking a question, or donating? The current design gives all three nearly equal intensity.
